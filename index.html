Four-step protocol for precise hair restoration

Illustrated overview of Micro-Extraction, Eco-Preservation, Precision Placement, and Rapid Recovery — optimized for clarity and patient education.

All illustrations are embedded as image assets in the images/ directory.

Micro-Extraction

Minimally invasive follicular removal using micro-tools; preserves surrounding tissue and reduces trauma.

Eco-Preservation

Environmentally-controlled handling to maintain graft viability and hydration during transfer.

Precision Placement

Accurate angulation and depth control — typically near a 45° insertion angle — for natural orientation and density.

Rapid Recovery

Optimized post-procedure care to minimize downtime and accelerate healing, often a 3–5 day recovery window.

Detailed description

Below are concise technical notes for each step. Use these for clinician reference or patient-facing educational material.

Micro-Extraction

Extraction with ultra-fine punches (typically 0.7–1.0 mm) targeting follicles at an angle matching native hair to avoid transection. Gentle hemostasis and minimal traction preserve graft integrity.

Eco-Preservation

Store grafts in isotonic, chilled solutions, minimize air exposure, and limit out-of-body time. Use cushioned handling trays and maintain 4–8°C for short-term storage when indicated.

Precision Placement

Use high-precision instruments, plan recipient sites with attention to hairline design, and insert grafts with correct depth and 30–45° angulation to match native pattern and minimize popping.

Rapid Recovery

Apply targeted cooling, short-term anti-inflammatories when appropriate, and provide patient instructions for wound care. Monitor for signs of infection and schedule follow-up within the first week.
